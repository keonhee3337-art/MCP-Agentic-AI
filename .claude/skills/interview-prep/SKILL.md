# Skill: Interview Prep

Structure prep for any interview — research, talking points, mock Q&A, and story framing.

## Trigger phrases
- "prep me for my interview at X"
- "what should I know about X company"
- "mock interview for X role"
- "help me answer [interview question]"
- "build my talking points for X"

## How to use
Give Claude the company, role, and any context you have. Examples:
- "Prep me for my Kearney AI RA interview — here's what I know about the role"
- "What should I know about Kearney as a consulting firm"
- "Mock interview — ask me consulting case questions focused on AI"
- "Help me answer: why do you want this role"

## What Claude does
1. **Research** — Looks up the company, role, and relevant industry context
2. **Talking points** — Builds a concise set of things to hit: why you, why them, your relevant experience
3. **Q&A prep** — Generates likely questions for the role and coaches answers
4. **Story framing** — Helps frame your AI projects and background in terms the interviewer cares about
5. **Mock Q&A** — Asks questions, you answer, Claude gives direct feedback

## Output format
- Bullet points throughout — no paragraphs unless drafting a written answer
- Talking points as a numbered list (prioritized)
- Mock Q&A clearly labeled: Q: / A: / Feedback:

## Notes
- Be direct about what you've already prepared vs. what you need help with
- Say "be harsh" for blunt feedback on answers
- Say "explain this like I'm talking to a non-AI person" to simplify AI jargon for interviewers unfamiliar with the space
- External tone applies here — professional, confident, no hedging
